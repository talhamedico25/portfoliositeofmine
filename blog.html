Dermatocosmetics vs acne medications for acne

draft • text-first • calm notes

Acne care sits on a spectrum: on one end, dermatocosmetics (cleansers, moisturizers, sunscreens, barrier support); on the other, medications (topicals and systemic therapies that target pathophysiology). Both matter — and the best results usually come from the right combination.

what dermatocosmetics do well

Dermatocosmetics reduce friction in a routine. They support adherence by making treatment tolerable.

  • Gentle cleansing that doesn’t strip the barrier
  • Non-comedogenic moisturizers to reduce irritation
  • Sunscreens that prevent post-inflammatory darkening
  • Simple routines that keep consistency high

what medications do well

Medications target the core drivers: comedones, inflammation, bacterial load, and sometimes hormones.

  • Retinoids for comedones and long-term control
  • Benzoyl peroxide for antimicrobial effect
  • Antibiotics (carefully) for inflammatory acne
  • Systemic options when severity warrants it

how to think about it (practical)

If your routine is harsh, you’ll quit early. If your routine is only cosmetic, you may plateau. The calm approach is to keep the base routine stable, then add the minimal effective medication.
